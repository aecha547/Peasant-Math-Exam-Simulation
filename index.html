Instructions and Information

  1. Answer all questions. Number your answers exactly as the questions are numbered.
  2. Read each source carefully before answering the questions that follow.
  3. Show calculations where required. Write the unit in your final answer.
  4. Use a ruler and pencil for all diagrams, graphs and mapwork.
  5. For bearings, measure clockwise from North and write three digits, for example 045°.
  6. Do not use content beyond Term 1 and Term 2 Grade 8 Geography.

Key Command Words

State / Name / Identify: give the answer only.
Calculate / Determine: work it out; show method.
Describe: say what it is like.
Explain: give a reason using because / therefore.
Compare: say what is similar and/or different.
Examine / Justify: use evidence from the source to support your answer.

Use after marking

This drill is deliberately short. It checks whether the learner can transfer knowledge, not just memorise answers.

  1. Explain, in two sentences, why Cape Town often has winter rainfall.
  2. Draw a tiny station model with 8/8 cloud cover and a north-easterly wind.
  3. On a 1 : 50 000 map, convert 9 cm to kilometres.
  4. Write one sentence using each word correctly: isobar, contour, latitude.
  5. Compare the terms weather and climate without using the word “temperature”.
