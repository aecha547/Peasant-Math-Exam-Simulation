The Top 5 Fixes From Her Marked Work

Fix What goes wrong Exam-safe method
1. Subtracting a negative She may do 24 - (-6) = 18. Change it to adding: 24 - (-6) → 24 + 6 = 30.
2. Hidden 1 A sign on its own feels confusing. -(1 + 1) → means -1(1 + 1) = -1 - 1 = -2.
3. Ratio sharing She may give the ratio parts instead of the actual amounts. Add parts first. For 3 : 5, total parts = 8.
4. Exponent laws She may calculate a power instead of leaving a single power. If same base multiplied, add powers: 34 × 32 = 36.
5. Algebra final signs She can group correctly but finish with -4y - 6y = -2y. Same sign → add sizes and keep sign: -4y - 6y = -10y.

Exam Writing Rules That Save Marks

  • Number exactly as the paper numbers the questions.
  • One line per step. Do not squeeze three steps into one messy line.
  • Use brackets for negative substitutions: write (-2)2, not -22.
  • Show the rule in the working: for HCF/LCM, show factors; for equations, show balancing steps.
  • If a final answer is a value of x, write x = ..., not just the number.

1. Whole Numbers, BODMAS, Factors, Multiples and Ratio

BODMAS / order of operations

When there are brackets, powers, multiplication/division, and addition/subtraction, work in this order:

Brackets  →  Powers / Roots  →  Multiply / Divide (Left to Right)  →  Add / Subtract (Left to Right)
Example Working Steps Answer
48 + 12 × 6 - 36 ÷ 9 48 + 72 - 4 → 120 - 4 116
32 + √81 × 2 - 4(5 - 2) 9 + 9 × 2 - 4 × 3 → 9 + 18 - 12 15

Prime factors, HCF and LCM

Prime factors are the prime numbers that multiply to make a number.

360 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 5 = 23 × 32 × 5
Term Meaning Quick test
Factor Goes exactly into a number. 6 is a factor of 24.
Multiple Answer in a times table. 24 is a multiple of 6.
HCF Biggest factor shared by both numbers. Useful for simplifying / dividing into equal groups.
LCM Smallest multiple shared by both numbers. Useful for common denominators and “divisible by both”.
Teacher Note on HCF/LCM Example:
Given lines: 84 = 22 × 3 × 7 and 126 = 2 × 32 × 7.
• HCF (Lowest common powers): 21 × 31 × 71 = 42
• LCM (Highest overall powers): 22 × 32 × 71 = 252

Ratio sharing

For 96 sweets shared in the ratio 3 : 5:

Total parts = 3 + 5 = 8 parts
Value of 1 part = 96 ÷ 8 = 12 sweets
Shares: (3 × 12)  :  (5 × 12) = 36 and 60
Trap: The answer is not 3 : 5 or 8 : 2. The question asks how many sweets each person receives.

2. Integers and Sign Rules

Integers include negative numbers, zero and positive numbers: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Rule Example Answer
Adding a negative means move down. 6 + (-4) 2
Subtracting a negative means add. 6 - (-4) 10
Negative × Negative = Positive. (-6)(-4) 24
Positive × Negative = Negative. 6(-4) -24

Brackets around powers matter

Written as Means Value
(-3)2 (-3) × (-3) 9
-32 -(3 × 3) -9

Worked integer examples

• (-12) + 18 - (-7) = 6 + 7 = 13
• (-6)(-4) - 30 ÷ (-5) = 24 - (-6) = 24 + 6 = 30
• (-3)2 - 32 + (-2)3 = 9 - 9 - 8 = -8
• Temperature change: -3 - 7 + 12 = -10 + 12 = 2°C
Must know: 24 - (-6) is not 18. It is 30.

4. Exponents and Scientific Notation

An exponent tells you how many times to multiply the base by itself.

Rule Example Result
Same base multiplied: add exponents. 34 × 32 36
Same base divided: subtract exponents. 105 ÷ 102 103
Power of a power: multiply exponents. (23)2 26
Negative base with odd power stays negative. (-3)3 -27
Negative base with even power becomes positive. (-3)2 9

Evaluate vs simplify

Evaluate means find the final numerical value: 25 = 32.

Simplify as a single power means leave it in exponent form: 34 × 32 = 36 (do not write 729 unless explicitly asked to evaluate).

Scientific notation

Scientific notation always has this exact format:

a × 10n,    where 1 ≤ a < 10
Ordinary Notation Scientific Notation
58 000 5.8 × 104
3 200 000 3.2 × 106
320 000 3.2 × 105

5. Patterns and Relationships

For a sequence, look for the constant difference between terms:

4;   7;   10;   13;   ...

The pattern values grow by adding 3 each time. The next two terms are 16 and 19.

The general formula rule is: multiply the position number n by 3, then add 1.

Tn = 3n + 1

The 20th term is calculated as: T20 = 3(20) + 1 = 61.

Flow diagrams

If given the mapping rule n → × 4 → +1, map out step-by-step inputs and outputs:

Input (n) Working Output
1 1 × 4 + 1 5
2 2 × 4 + 1 9
5 5 × 4 + 1 21
Good sign: This is one of her strong topics. She should keep it neat and not overthink it.

6. Algebraic Expressions

Expression vs equation

Expression Equation
3x + 7 3x + 7 = 19
No equals sign. Has an equals sign.
Simplify structural terms or evaluate. Solve for the specific unknown value.

Vocabulary definitions

For the expression: 9 + 3x2 - 4x3 - 4x

Word Meaning Applied Examples
Term Parts separated strictly by + or - signs. 9,  3x2,  -4x3,  -4x (4 terms)
Coefficient The numerical value multiplying the variable. Of x2 is 3;   Of x is -4.
Constant A standalone number without any variable. 9

Arranged in descending powers (highest exponent first):

-4x3 + 3x2 - 4x + 9

Substitution methods

If substituting the negative value x = -1, protect it with brackets:

9 + 3(-1)2 - 4(-1)3 - 4(-1)
→ 9 + 3(1) - 4(-1) + 4
→ 9 + 3 + 4 + 4 = 20
Trap: Always bracket negative values when substituting.

Algebraic Expressions Continued

Writing expressions from words

Word Phrase Algebraic Expression
The sum of a and b a + b
The product of a and b ab
Three times the difference between p and q 3(p - q)
The sum of a number and double the same number n + 2n = 3n

Simplifying like terms

Only combine terms sharing matching variable bases and matching exponents.

Expression Grouping Steps Answer
3a - 2a - 5a (3 - 2 - 5)a -4a
7m - 4n + 3m + 2n (7m + 3m) + (-4n + 2n) 10m - 2n

Multiplying algebraic terms

Multiply coefficients together first, then add the variable exponents.

x2 × 2x3 × 5x4 = (1 × 2 × 5)x2+3+4 = 10x9

Expanding brackets (Distributive law)

Multiply the outer factor across every single element inside the bracket:

3(x - y) - 2(2x - y) - 3x
→ 3x - 3y - 4x + 2y - 3x
→ (3x - 4x - 3x) + (-3y + 2y) = -4x - y
Final sign check: -3y + 2y = -y, not -5y.

8. Geometry of Angles

Geometric Definition Core Structural Meaning Critical Context Reminder
Complementary angles Two angles that add up to exactly 90°. Complement of 37° is 90° - 37° = 53°.
Supplementary angles Two angles that add up to exactly 180°. Supplement of 112° is 180° - 112° = 68°.
Vertically opposite angles Opposite individual angles formed by two intersecting lines. They are exactly equal to each other.
Adjacent angles Angles side-by-side sharing a vertex and one arm. They sit touching each other closely.
Angles on a straight line Angles aligned along a flat continuous plane line. They sum to a total of 180°.
Angles around a point Angles filling a total full-circle rotation. They sum to a total of 360°.

Worked execution examples

If angles spanning a complete point are given as 70°, 110°, 95° and x:

x = 360° - (70° + 110° + 95°) = 360° - 275° = 85°

If two touching adjacent angles sitting on a straight line are defined as 2× + 30° and 3× - 10°:

(2x + 30°) + (3x - 10°) = 180°   [angles on a straight line]
5x + 20° = 180°
5x = 160° → x = 32
Trap: If the question asks for x, the final answer is x = 32, not 180°.

Final 10-Minute Checklist Before the Exam

  • BODMAS: Brackets, powers/roots, multiply/divide, add/subtract.
  • Subtracting negative: a - (-b) = a + b.
  • Squaring negatives: (-3)2 = 9, but -32 = -9.
  • HCF vs LCM: HCF = biggest shared factor. LCM = smallest shared multiple.
  • Ratio sharing: Add parts, find one part, multiply back.
  • Fractions: Add/subtract need common denominator. Divide means keep-change-flip.
  • Exponents: Multiply same base = add powers. Divide same base = subtract powers. Power of power = multiply powers.
  • Algebra terms: Only combine like terms.
  • Substitution: Put negative values in brackets.
  • Equations: Keep both sides balanced and show every step.
  • Geometry: straight line = 180°, around a point = 360°, complement = 90°, supplement = 180°.
  • Presentation: If a marker cannot read it, it cannot earn full marks. Write neatly.
Final reminder: She is not cooked. Her marked paper shows she can do the difficult parts. The exam goal is to slow down just enough to protect the marks she already knows how to earn.
